Leadership Theory & Practice (3 credits)

What makes a good leader? In this course, Dr. Myers begins with a Biblical understanding of leadership and takes you on a guided tour of leadership ideas through history. Along the way, you'll develop financial and time management skills and hear from influential modern day leaders like Chuck Colson, Star Parker, David Barton, and Voddie Baucham. Before the journey is over, you'll learn to set realistic goals and build a personal plan for leadership.

Course Topics:

  • Gratitude as a foundation of leadership
  • The history of leadership
  • Are leaders born or cultivated?
  • What's more important than IQ?
  • How to develop successful goals
  • Improve your time management
  • Develop a personal budget
  • Is ambition good or bad?
  • Discovering your life stories
  • Create a leadership plan
  • How to find personal mentors

Leadership and Management Communication (3 credits)

Relationships are impossible without communication. Many bright and talented leaders have been stunted in their influence by an inability to clearly and graciously communicate their ideas to others. This course explores the challenges that leaders face while attempting to communicate to their stakeholders.

Course Topics:

  • How to get others to take you seriously
  • Communicating with others who don't share your values
  • The science of persuasion
  • Being above reproach
  • What makes a good story
  • How to motivate others
  • Developing listening skills
  • Building a network of mentors
  • Conquering apathy
  • Ethical principles of communication
  • Propaganda and manipulation
  • Conflict resolution and peacemaking
  • Responding to criticism
  • Negotiating effectively
